60-second summary

  • Optimise for recall: in this workflow, missing relevant studies is usually worse than reading extra irrelevant ones.
  • Treat scores as ranking first: the model helps decide what to screen next, not what to auto-include.
  • Outputs evolve as labels arrive: ranking and residual-risk estimates are expected to change during active learning.
  • Simulation outputs are planning aids: they are scenario-based estimates, not guaranteed truth.
  • Use staged decisions: default policy is +50 screens, then reassess before escalating.

Key concepts (plain language)

Active learning loop

Seed labels -> rank queue -> screen -> retrain -> repeat. Metrics are moving snapshots during this loop.

Ranking vs classification

Primary objective is useful ordering of records. A fixed threshold (like 0.5) can look weak even when ranking is useful.

Precision vs recall trade-off

Lower thresholds usually raise recall and lower precision. In evidence screening, this can be the right trade.

WSS@95 and workload

WSS@95 estimates work saved at high recall. It can be negative when high-recall retrieval is very expensive.

How to interpret outputs

Use this decision lens when reading project outputs and planner scenarios:

Output What it means How to use it
Average Precision (AP) How well relevant studies are ranked near the top. Prioritise AP when deciding if the queue is useful for reviewer time.
Recall / Recall@k How many relevant studies are found overall or within top-k. Use recall as the primary safety metric for stopping decisions.
Precision / Precision@k How many surfaced records are truly relevant. Treat as workload signal, not sole quality verdict.
FN/FP scenario estimates Estimated misses/over-inclusions under explicit assumptions. Compare scenarios to choose +50 vs +100 stages.
Cap reached Requested additional screens exceed remaining records. Do not overinterpret large increments when cap truncates effective additions.

Interpretation rule of thumb

Ask: "Does this extra screening step reduce expected false negatives enough to justify reviewer effort?"

Common pitfalls

  • Over-trusting a single threshold metric at 0.5.
  • Assuming planner outputs are certainty rather than assumption-based scenarios.
  • Ignoring prevalence sensitivity when comparing FN/FP estimates.
  • Skipping reconciliation checks before re-running analyses.
  • Escalating directly to large screening jumps instead of staged reassessment.

Model behavior and thresholds
  • Baseline: TF-IDF + logistic regression (class balanced).
  • Current improved winner: calibrated linear SVM with word + character TF-IDF features.
  • Thresholds are tuned for recall targets (~90% and ~95%) and can shift between runs.
  • Ranking metrics and threshold metrics can move differently; this is normal.
Simulation assumptions and uncertainty
  • Historical labels are treated as ground truth for simulation.
  • Prevalence bands (low/medium/high) change expected FN/FP burdens.
  • Scenario outputs are deterministic arithmetic conditional on those assumptions.
  • As the screened pool changes, previous recommendations can become stale.
Decision playbook checkpoints
  1. Before +50: validate queue integrity, unique IDs, and prior reconciliation status.
  2. After +50: refresh artifacts and compare residual FN estimate against tolerance.
  3. Escalate to +100 only when assurance requirements or residual risk justify it.

Glossary

Active learning
Iterative process where reviewed labels update the model and reprioritise what to screen next.
Average Precision (AP)
Ranking-focused summary that rewards placing relevant studies near the top.
False negative (FN)
A relevant study not retrieved at the current stopping decision.
False positive (FP)
A non-relevant study surfaced as potentially relevant.
Prevalence
Estimated share of truly relevant studies in the full candidate pool.
WSS@95
Work saved over random sampling when targeting 95% recall.
